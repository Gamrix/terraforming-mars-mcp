## What Went Wrong

### 1. AWARDS SWEPT AGAIN: 0 VP vs 15 VP (–15 VP)
4th consecutive game with 0 awards. This time I DID fund Scientist (Gen 11 at 7-6 lead), but John played Adaptation Technology in Gen 12 for +1 science tag, tying 8-8. Scientist went to John as "first to reach" tiebreaker or the final score at 8 vs 7 — either way I lost.

**Root cause**: Scientist is VOLATILE. Science tags can be added right up to game end via events like Adaptation Technology, Technology Demonstration, Fusion Power, Research. John had 23 cards in hand from his AI Central + Business Network engine and could pull a science tag at will.

**Key lesson**: Funding Scientist requires a 3+ tag lead OR being unable to catch up. A 1-tag lead at 7-6 Gen 11 with 4 more gens was NOT safe. In retrospect should have funded Landlord (lost by same margin) or not funded at all.

### 2. 3-MILESTONE CAP BLOCKED MAYOR (–5 VP)
Built 3 cities (Immigrant City 35, Research Outpost 21, SP City 37) by Gen 12, but Terraformer + Planner + Gardener were already all claimed. Mayor milestone impossible to claim.

**Root cause**: John claimed Planner at 16 cards in Gen 6 (I couldn't prevent — his card draw engine got there fast). Then claimed Gardener at 3 greeneries in Gen 10. With my own Terraformer, 3 milestones claimed = cap reached. Mayor dead.

**Key lesson**: Track milestone cap. If 2 milestones already claimed, the 3rd-milestone slot becomes a race. Need to anticipate which milestone the opponent can claim fastest and either race it OR abandon my 3rd milestone plan.

### 3. ECONOMY GAP: 30 MC prod vs 47 MC prod
Better than Game 5's 20 vs 61, but still huge disparity. John's engine: Teractor base, Allied Bank +4, Space Hotels +4, Toll Station +8 (!!), Miranda Resort +7 (Earth tag scaling), Callisto +3, Food Factory +4, Farming +2, Tundra +2. Total ~47 MC prod.

Mine: Satellites +5, Io MI +2, Immigration Shuttles +5, Immigrant City +1, Noctis Farming +1, Lightning Harvest +1, Zeppelins +3 (3 cities), UNMI bonus. Total ~30 MC prod. Plus -2 from John's Hackers.

**Key lesson**: Toll Station (+1 MC per OCEAN placement) and Miranda Resort (+1 MC per Earth tag) were game-warping for John. Need to identify these scaling MC cards and hate-draft them if possible.

### 4. CITY ADJACENCY GAP: 8 vs 20 (–12 VP)
My cities (35, 37, 21) had 8 adjacent greeneries total. John's cities (42 Capital, 09, 56, 48, 39, 58 Space Station + 01/02 off-board) had 20 adjacencies. Capital alone scored ~6 VP from clustering.

**Root cause**:
1. Only 3 cities on Mars vs John's 7.
2. My RO at 21 was isolated — took 3 greeneries to get adjacency VP; slow buildup.
3. John placed cities AFTER mine, specifically next to my greeneries (city at 39 adjacent to my greenery 30 — +1 VP for HIM).

**Key lesson**: Need 4–5+ cities on Mars. Rush SP City once cap of 3 milestones is hit and Mayor is blocked — the 25 MC for a city still gives 3–5 VP from adjacency.

### 5. John's multi-card VP-per-resource engine dwarfed mine
- Ecological Zone: VP per 2 animals, AND gains animal on every plant/animal tag played. John has 9 plant tags + 4 animal tags = 13 triggers = 13 animals = 6 VP. This card alone beat my Ants (7 VP) while costing less.
- Herbivores: +1 animal per greenery placed (any player's). John placed 9+ greeneries = 9+ animals = 4+ VP. Plus mine counted too.
- Pets: animal per city placed = 7 animals = 3 VP.

Key insight: Ecological Zone and Herbivores have EFFECT triggers (not just action slots), so they accumulate far more than Ants or Birds which require spending actions.

## Key Lessons

1. **Scientist award is VOLATILE.** A 1-tag lead is never safe when opponent has card draw engines. Fund only with 3+ tag lead and few expected science cards coming.

2. **Watch the 3-milestone cap.** Once 2 are claimed and you don't have a 3rd you can lock down, the remaining 2 slots become a sprint. If Mayor is blocked, stop building cities for milestone — pivot to cities for adjacency VP only.

3. **Ecological Zone + Herbivores > Ants + Birds.** John's VP-per-resource cards had EFFECT triggers (auto-accumulate on plays) while mine needed ACTIONS (limited 1/gen). When these cards appear in draft, TAKE THEM even if not natural fit — they're broken.

4. **Toll Station and Miranda Resort are game-winning MC scalers.** Hate-draft when seen. Toll = +1 MC per ocean placed (game = 9 oceans = 9 MC triggers). Miranda = +1 MC per Earth tag (John had 7 Earth tags).

5. **John's 60+ card play rate continues to be decisive.** His AI Central + Business Network + Olympus Conference draw engine let him play 2-3x more cards per gen. Need to invest earlier in card draw (Mars University, Inventors' Guild, AI Central) to match throughput.

6. **PhoboLog + Satellites + Io MI is a viable S-tier opening.** Satellites at 10 MC for +5 MC prod was game-making. Io MI's Ti prod synergizes with PhoboLog's Ti value. When drafting these cards early, the engine compounds.

7. **Final-gen conversion saved VP.** The post-production greenery conversion (plants → greenery at space 14 adj to RO) gave +2 VP (tile + adj). Don't waste plants — always convert at game end.

## Strategic Pivot for Game 7

Three persistent structural problems across 5+ games:
- **Awards: 0 VP in 5 straight games.** Consider strategy: fund only awards with STRUCTURAL lead (10+ advantage) that can't flip, OR completely ignore awards as a category.
- **City adjacency: 8–10 VP stuck.** Need to aggressively use SP City (25 MC) mid-game for 4–5 cities, not just 3.
- **Opposing VP-per-resource engines.** Need to hate-draft Ecological Zone, Herbivores, Pets if they appear, OR draft them first.
