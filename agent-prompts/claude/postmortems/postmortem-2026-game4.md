### 5. Greenery VP: 10 vs 9

## What Went Wrong

### 1. AWARDS SWEPT: 0 VP vs 15 VP (–15 VP swing)

John funded all 3 awards (Thermalist 8 MC, Miner 14 MC, Scientist 14 MC) and won all three:
- **Thermalist**: John 11 heat vs my 4. His 7 heat prod dominated.
- **Miner**: John 8 (steel+titanium) vs my 5. His 4 steel + 4 Ti prod gave him resources.
- **Scientist**: John 8 science tags vs my 3. Mars University + AI Central + multiple science cards.

**Root cause**: I did not fund any awards myself, and I was not competitive in any of the categories John funded. I never built heat production (0 heat prod all game), had low science tags, and low steel/titanium resources at end.

**Lesson**: Fund at least 1 award where I have a strong lead. In this game, Landlord (most tiles) or Banker (MC production) might have been contestable. Even funding an award at 8 MC that I win is 5 VP for 8 MC = 1.6 MC/VP, the same rate as milestones.

### 2. CITY ADJACENCY GAP: 10 vs 20 (–10 VP)

John had 5 cities vs my 3:
- John: Capital (41), Lava Tube Settlement (09), Underground City (47), city at 11, Noctis City (31) — all placed near greenery clusters
- Claude: city at 24, city at 35 (Immigrant City), city at 19 (SP, placed Gen 12)

John strategically placed cities late-game adjacent to existing greeneries:
- City 11 at (6,1): adjacent to 3 of MY greeneries (04, 16, 17) — stole 3 adjacency VP from my tiles
- City 31 (Noctis): adjacent to 3 of his greeneries
- City 47 (Underground): adjacent to 3 of his greeneries

**Root cause**: I only had 2 city cards (Immigrant City, Rover Construction city bonus). John had 5 city plays including SP City. More cities = more adjacency VP, especially when placed near existing greenery clusters. I also placed greeneries without considering future opponent city adjacency.

**Lesson**: 
1. Place more cities — 3 cities is too few. Target 4-5 cities minimum.
2. Consider SP City earlier when greenery clusters form.
3. Place greeneries defensively — avoid creating clusters that an opponent city can exploit for adjacency.

### 3. TR GAP: 44 vs 49 (–5 VP)

John raised TR more through:
- Nitrite Reducing Bacteria: remove 3 microbes → +1 TR (used twice = +2 TR)
- Convert Heat: used multiple times with 7+ heat production
- Imported Nitrogen: +1 TR
- Vitor bonus: +3 MC on every VP card = reinvested into more plays

**Root cause**: John's 40 MC production economy funded more actions per generation. I couldn't match his card throughput (he played 56 cards to my 38). More plays = more TR opportunities.

### 4. ECONOMY GAP: MC prod 20 vs 40

John's MC production doubled mine by endgame:
- Vitor +3 MC per VP card played (~15 VP cards = 45 MC bonus over game)
- Capital (+5 MC prod from adjacent oceans)
- Zeppelins (+4 MC prod from 4 cities)
- Noctis City (+3 MC prod)
- Multiple other prod cards (Cartel, Space Hotels, Tropical Resort)

My MC production was 13-20 range, mostly from Immigration Shuttles (+5) and Immigrant City triggers.

**Root cause**: I focused on plant production and VP accumulation over MC production. This limited my ability to play cards and fund awards in the late game. Need better balance between VP engine and economic engine.

## Key Lessons

1. **Awards cannot be ignored.** Zero awards in back-to-back games is a –10 to –15 VP hole each time. Fund at least 1 award where you have a strong or growing lead. Even winning 1 award (5 VP for 8 MC) transforms the awards category from –15 to –5.

2. **City count matters enormously for adjacency VP.** Each city = potential 3-6 adjacency VP from surrounding greeneries. Having 3 cities vs 5 cities is a structural disadvantage. Target 4-5 cities per game through city cards + SP City.

3. **Greenery placement must consider opponent city adjacency.** Placing greeneries in a line or cluster creates a target for an opponent to place a city adjacent to multiple tiles. Spread greeneries around your own cities, not in open areas exploitable by opponent cities.

4. **VP-per-resource card strategy is validated.** Going from 15 card VP (Game 3) to 35 card VP (Game 4) was the biggest improvement. Continue prioritizing Ecological Zone, Birds, Fish, Small Animals, Ants, and similar VP accumulators.

5. **Economy needs to keep pace.** 20 MC prod vs 40 MC prod means half the purchasing power. Need at least 25+ MC production by endgame to stay competitive on cards, SP cities, and award funding.

6. **Wild tags have valuable Jovian synergy.** Research Network's wild tag counted as Jovian for Terraforming Ganymede. Look for wild tag + Jovian card combos.
